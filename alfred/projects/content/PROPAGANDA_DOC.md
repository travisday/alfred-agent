# PROPAGANDA STRATEGY BLUEPRINT

**Purpose:**  
Turn content into a system that helps people gain leverage — so they can get better jobs, build startups, ship MVPs, pitch investors, and improve their financial freedom.

This is not about coding for its own sake.  
This is about **using AI to lower barriers, build leverage faster, and create real options**.

While my content on the surface focuses on using AI to build software, the core desires of my audience are to gain freedom & leverage to improve their status.

All content should tie back to the core mission of helping people gain leverage and freedom.

---

## SECTION 1 — From Posting to Shaping Outcomes

### Big Mission
Help people use AI to build real applications **without needing to become good at coding**, while still learning the fundamentals required to ship, deploy, and maintain software.

The goal is leverage:
- leverage in the job market
- leverage to build and pitch MVPs
- leverage to build your own company

### Cultural Shift
- "You need years of coding first" → **"You can build now if you learn the right things"**
- "AI does everything" → **"AI changes what you need to learn"**
- "Coding skill = freedom" → **"Leverage = freedom"**
- "Content about AI = only tutorials" → **"Content about AI = tutorials AND lifestyle proof"**

## SECTION 2 — Decide the Battle You’re Fighting

### Worldview to Install
**AI and vibe coding make building apps much easier —  
but you still need to learn fundamentals and use tools correctly.  
If you do, you gain leverage.**

### Identity / Behavior Shift
From:
- “I’m blocked because I can’t code well”
- “I need years before I can build something real”
- “This isn’t accessible to me”

To:
- **“I can build usable software by learning fundamentals and using AI correctly.”**
- **“I can turn ideas into leverage.”**

### What You Are NOT Trying to Do
1. Turn people into elite low-level engineers  
2. Pretend fundamentals don’t matter  
3. Sell “no learning required” fantasies

## SECTION 3 — Map the Current Battlefield (Audience Frames)

### What stories, myths, or narratives dominate your niche right now?
- AI will replace engineers.
- Vibe coding means you don’t need to learn anything.
- If you didn’t learn coding the hard way, it doesn’t count.
- You need years of experience before you can build something real.
- AI-built apps are demos, not real products.

### Audience fears, myths, and misconceptions
- Fear of not being technical enough to build anything meaningful.
- Fear of wasting time learning the wrong skills.
- Belief that coding skill is still the main bottleneck.
- Confusion about what fundamentals actually matter now.
- Belief that AI either does everything or ruins everything.

### Core tension
They believe they need years of coding experience before they can improve their situation.

You know that AI makes building apps accessible much earlier — but only if people still learn fundamentals and use tools correctly. That leverage can directly change their job prospects, income, or ability to start a company.

### Language your audience already uses
- Build apps
- MVP
- Deploy
- Production
- It works locally
- AI tools
- One-shot app
- Vibe coding (note: limits reach to insiders — use broader terms first in hooks and CTAs)

### Existing enemy in their mind
- Overcomplicated learning paths
- Gatekeeping advice
- “Learn everything first” narratives
- AI hype that ignores reality


## SECTION 4 — Forge Your Narrative

### Core framing statements
- AI makes building apps easier, not automatic.
- You don’t need to be good at coding — you need to understand software.
- Fundamentals still matter. They just moved.
- Building apps is leverage.

### Symbols and identity
- Practical stacks like Cursor, Supabase, Clerk, and Railway
- Real production examples instead of polished demos
- Calm, explanatory tone
- No hype, no shortcuts, no shaming

### Story Template A (Educational)
- Villain: unnecessary barriers, outdated advice, false extremes
- Hero: people who want better options and more freedom
- Guide: you, explaining what actually matters now
- Victory: shipped apps, better jobs, MVPs, real leverage

### Story Template B (Lifestyle Proof)
- Scene: a moment from Travis's life (building from a rooftop, casual hot take while cooking, celebrating a launch)
- Character: Travis as living proof, not teacher
- Tension: "How does he live like this?"
- Resolution: implied — he builds with AI

### Visual cues
- B-roll with clear text
- Dashboards, terminals, configs
- Simple visuals over flashy edits

### What your brand is and is not
- This is about leverage and freedom.

- This is not about skipping responsibility.

### Core contrasts you use
- Code vs systems
- Demo vs production

- Idea vs leverage
- Hype vs reality

### Emotions to evoke first
- Relief: I don't need years before I can start.
- Clarity: Now I know what matters.
- Confidence: This is achievable.
- Motivation: I should build something.
- Aspiration: "I want that life."
- Belonging: "I could be part of that."

### How you evoke these emotions

- Show lifestyle without explaining it — let the moment speak.
- Share milestones without making them instructional.
- Let the viewer project themselves into the scene.

## SECTION 10 — Core Propaganda Pillars

### Lifestyle
Working in a high paying job, building apps on the side, working out and enjoying life.

Lifestyle is both a **topic** and a **format**. It's the most undertapped pillar — 3 of the top 5 all-time talking heads are lifestyle content, but 0 of the recent 50 reels are.

**Specific moments to capture:**
- Building from interesting locations (rooftop, coffee shop, travel)
- Casual hot takes while cooking, walking, or working out
- Hackathon and milestone events (app launch, first user, first paying customer)
- Apps running in the background while Travis does something else

**How to use it:**
- Show, don't teach. The moment IS the content.
- Let the connection to building with AI be implied or one sentence max.
- Optimize for shares and follows, not saves.
- Use Story Template B from Section 4.
