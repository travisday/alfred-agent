## WHAT 10K+ TALKING HEADS WOULD LOOK LIKE NOW

Based on what worked and what's changed, here are data-backed hypotheses:

### 1. New Blueprint Territory

Your stack reel dominated because nobody had done "here's the complete cheap stack to build apps" before. You need to find the NEXT blueprint that hasn't been done to death.

**Possible new blueprints (untested by you):**
- "This is how I go from app idea to first paying user" (the full monetization path, not just deployment)
- "This is how I built [specific app] in [timeframe] and it makes $X/month" (blueprint + proof)
- "This is how I use AI at my 9-to-5 vs my side projects" (two workflows, career angle)
- "This is how I automated [specific personal task] and saved [specific hours]" (personal automation blueprint)

The key: it has to be a system the viewer has NOT seen from you before.

### 2. Story-First Talking Heads

Your ceviche, hackathon, and beach reels prove that story reels drive views. You need to bring this energy back.

**Possible story angles:**
- Building/shipping something live with a real outcome
- A "day in my life" as a software engineer who vibe codes
- Real moments: a win, a failure, a pivot, a surprise
- Behind the scenes of your actual work (9-to-5 or startup)

The key: these don't need to be educational. They need to make people FEEL something and share.

### 3. The Hybrid (Biggest Opportunity)

Combine a new blueprint with a story. Your 517K reel was close to this (it walked through a real process) but was purely instructional.

**What a hybrid looks like:**
- "I built an app this weekend that [outcome]. Here's exactly how." (story + blueprint)
- "A founder came to me with an idea. 48 hours later it was live. Here's the stack." (story + blueprint)
- "I automated my entire [morning/workflow/content process]. This is the setup." (lifestyle + blueprint)

The key: start with the OUTCOME or MOMENT, then reveal the system.

## SUMMARY: WHAT THE DATA SAYS TO DO

**Double down on:**
1. **How-to/process/stack content** with specific, save-worthy information
2. **"This is the [X] way to [outcome]" hook formula** — it's your best performing pattern
3. **Reels where someone walks away with something they can reference or use**

**Reduce:**
1. **Comment response volume** — 42% of slots, 16% of saves. The math doesn't work. Keep 1-2 per week if they have real emotional charge, cut the rest.
2. **Abstract takes and opinion reels** — "AI is going to get better" and "use the right tool" don't give people anything to save or share.

**Test:**
1. **Substack bridge CTAs** on your top-performing content types. You have zero data on this because you've never tried it. Your how-to reels create the perfect "I want more" moment. Test whether "full breakdown in my newsletter, link in bio" outperforms "follow me for more tips" on those reels.
2. **Shorter scripts on how-to reels.** Your best reels work at any length when the content is dense. But test whether a tighter 80-word version of a stack reel gets the same save rate with higher completion.
3. **Reframing tool comparisons as outcome-first hooks.** "Supabase or Firebase" works for existing audience but limits reach. Test "This is the cheapest database for your first app" (same content, wider hook) and compare.
4. **Language experimentation.** "Vibe coding" appears in nearly every reel. Your b-roll (which may use different language) gets 2.5x the views. Test talking heads that use "build apps with AI" instead and compare reach to non-followers.
