Subject: 3 questions that would've saved me months of wasted building

The first app I vibed coded I ended up deleting 90% of the code, not because the code was bad but becasue I  was building a bunch of useless features that didn't actually matter.

I see this same problem all the time with new builders.

Because buidling with AI is so fast and easy its also very easy to keep building shit you don't need.

So if you're just having fun and learning, then go bananas. 

But if you're actually trying to build something useful and not waste a bunch of time and tokens then you need to ask yourself these 3 questions.

### 1. What does it do?

Can you describe it in one sentence?

If you can't do that, the idea isn't clear enough yet.

And if *you* can't describe it clearly, AI definitely can't build it either.

"It helps freelancers track unpaid invoices and send reminders." That's clear. That's buildable.

"It's like a dashboard for everything in my business." That's a rabbit hole. You'll be building for three days and still not know what the app does.

### 2. Who is it for?

There are really only three answers here. You, a specific person you know, or a specific group of people with a shared problem.

"Everyone" is not an answer.

And honestly building for yourself is super underrated. No users to worry about. No deployment pressure. Nobody complaining when something breaks.

Some of my most useful apps have one user (me).

### 3. What's the smallest version that's useful?

This is the big one.

Take your idea and strip it down. Remove features until you're left with the one thing that makes it actually useful.

"Track invoices and send reminders" becomes → "A list of invoices with due dates and a button to send a reminder email."

No analytics dashboard. No client portal. No Stripe integration. Just the core thing.

You can always add more later.

Scope creep kills more projects than bad code ever will.


Learn from my mistakes and don't waste your time building something you don't need.
