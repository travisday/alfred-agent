# Behind the Scenes: Most of My Apps Have Zero Users

**Type:** Behind the Scenes / Lessons Learned (Post Type 3)
**Day:** Friday
**Target Length:** 500-800 words

## Subject Line

"Nobody uses my apps. That's the whole point."

## Hook

People assume that if you're building apps, you're building startups. Or at least products. Something with users, a landing page, maybe a waitlist. That's one path. But it's not the only one — and honestly, it's not even the one I use most.

Most of the apps I build have zero users. And that's the point.

## Outline

### The Assumption
- When people hear "build apps" they picture a startup or a product launch
- There's this implicit pressure that every app needs to be deployed, have users, and go somewhere
- That pressure stops people from building anything at all

### What I Actually Build Most
- Personal tools that live on my laptop and solve my specific problems
- Share 2-3 specific real examples:
  - A script that organizes files/folders automatically
  - A tool that processes data I work with regularly
  - A simple automation that handles a repetitive task
- None of these have users. None of them are deployed. None of them need to be polished.
- They just work for me. And collectively, they save me hours every week.

### Why This Is the Best Way to Learn
- Low stakes: if it breaks, nobody cares but you
- Real motivation: you're solving an actual problem you have
- Immediate feedback: you know instantly if it works because you use it
- No deployment anxiety: you don't need to worry about security, scale, auth, or any of the things that make "real" apps intimidating
- Builds the same core skills: describing what you want, testing, debugging, iterating

### How Personal Tools Led to Bigger Things
- Every skill I use professionally started with something small I built for myself
- Understanding how a database works → started with a personal tool that stored data
- Understanding deployment → eventually wanted to share a tool with a friend
- The skills compound, but you don't have to start at "production-ready"

### The Permission Slip
- If you've been wanting to build but feel intimidated by the idea of launching something — don't launch anything
- Build something that solves one annoying problem in your day
- That's it. That's enough. That's real.
