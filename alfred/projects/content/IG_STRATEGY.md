# INSTAGRAM CONTENT STRATEGY

**For High Performers, Entrepreneurs, and Business-Minded People**

> Mission: Turn Instagram into a follow-generating engine that helps high performers realize they can build apps to gain leverage — without becoming engineers.

**North Star Metric: Follows.** Every structural decision in this document optimizes for earning new followers. Saves and shares are leading indicators by format. Views without follows mean the content entertained but didn't convert.

### What Makes Them Follow

People follow from two signals. Everything else generates views without conversion.

1. **Proof that someone like them built something real** (Story reels) — they see Travis living a life they want and connect it to building with AI. The follow is aspirational: "I need to see more of this."
2. **A complete system they want to reference later** (Blueprint reels) — they see a stack, workflow, or framework they can't fully absorb in 30 seconds. The follow is practical: "I need to come back to this."
3. **A CTA that promises continued specific value** — "Follow me for more on building apps with AI" converts. "Follow for more" doesn't.
4. **They do NOT follow from clap-backs** — defensive/comment-response content generates comments and views but near-zero follow signal. Data: 42% of recent content was comment responses with almost no saves or shares.

## LANGUAGE & POSITIONING

### What to Say

**USE THIS LANGUAGE:**
- "Build apps" (not "vibe code apps")
- "AI coding agents" (not "vibe coding")
- "Entrepreneurs" and "founders" (not "non-techies")
- "MVPs" and "working products" (concrete outcomes)
- "Start now" (their actual goal)
- "Test your idea" (their actual goal)
- "In 2026" / "now" (feels current, not fringe)

### "Vibe Coding" Usage (3-Tier System)

- **Hooks & CTAs:** Never use "vibe coding." Lead with "build apps with AI", "AI coding agents."
- **Body/Context:** Acceptable when referencing the practice mid-reel (e.g., "what some people call vibe coding is really just...").
- **CTAs:** Replace "follow me for more vibe coding tips" with "follow me for more on building apps with AI."

**FORMATTING:**
- Never use em dashes. Use periods for sentence breaks instead.
- Keep hooks and b-roll text clean. Short sentences separated by periods.

### Core Messages (Repeat These)

1. **AI changed what you need to learn** — not whether you need to learn
2. **You don't need to code, but you need to understand basic tools to build software** — fundamentals matter
3. **Building apps is leverage** — it's not coding for coding's sake
4. **Speed matters** — ship MVPs before competitors, prove ideas before fundraising
5. **Learn skills, not specific platforms** — tools change, workflows don't
6. **Building with AI is a lifestyle, not just a skill** — it changes how you work, create, and live

## REEL STRUCTURES

Two structures. Every reel uses one or the other. One concept per reel. 25-30 seconds max.

### Structure A: The Blueprint

For reels that teach, explain, or give a system. Optimized for saves.

| Section | What to Say | Purpose |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Hook** | One sentence using a proven hook formula. No setup. Start mid-statement. | Stop the scroll |
| **Credential** | "My name is Travis. I'm a software engineer." | Establish authority |
| **One Concept** | Deliver ONE concept. Remove ONE barrier. Bust ONE myth. Give ONE step. Plain English. One analogy max. | Shift the belief |
| **Outcome Tie** | ONE sentence connecting this to career/money/freedom/leverage/speed. Make it concrete. Can be implicit for blueprints where the system IS the outcome. | Show the prize |
| **CTA** | Either a **Follow CTA** ("Follow me for more on building apps with AI") or a **Bridge CTA** ("I break this down step by step in my newsletter. Link in bio."). Never both. | Earn the follow or drive to Substack |

**Outcome tie options (pick one per reel):**
- "...which is why I doubled my comp doing this."
- "...so you can test your startup idea for under $50."
- "...which means you can build your MVP this weekend instead of searching for a co-founder."
- "...because investors fund MVPs, not pitch decks."
- "...which is how entrepreneurs are switching to technical roles."
- "...so you're not stuck waiting on developers to execute your ideas."
- "...which is the fastest path from idea to income."
- "...because speed matters when beating competitors to market."
- "...which saves me 10+ hours per week."

### Structure B: The Story

For reels that show lifestyle, milestones, or moments. Optimized for shares and follows.

| Section | What to Say | Purpose |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Hook** | A moment or scene, not a teaching frame. Visual or situational. | Stop the scroll with curiosity |
| **Scene** | 5-15 seconds of the moment. Casual first-person energy. Show, don't teach. | Let them project into the life |
| **Pivot** | ONE sentence connecting the moment to building with AI. Lighter than an outcome tie. | Link lifestyle to skill |
| **CTA** | Always a Follow CTA. Never a Bridge CTA. Story reels sell the person, not the newsletter. | Earn the follow |

**Example pivot lines:**
- "This is what happens when you can build your own tools."
- "Not bad for a Tuesday."
- "Building with AI bought me this."

## CONTENT FORMATS & TYPES

Two formats. Five types. This replaces the old 7-type system.

### Format 1: Blueprint Reels (Save Machine)

Blueprints teach. They give the viewer a system, process, or belief shift they want to save and reference. Use Structure A.

**Type 1 — Stack/Workflow**
Complete system with named tools. Must contain NEW information.

- Hook patterns: "This is the [cheapest/fastest] way to [outcome]." / "These are the [#] tools you need." / "Steal my [workflow/stack] for [result]."
- Examples: cheapest way to build and launch an app, my exact workflow from idea to deployed app, personal automation stack
- CTA: Follow CTA or Bridge CTA
- Leading indicator: saves

**Type 2 — How-To/Process**
Solves one specific problem with a clear process.

- Hook patterns: "If you have [situation], do this first." / "This is the actual first step to [outcome]." / "Before you [wrong thing], do this instead."
- Examples: how to plan an app before touching any tool, answer these 2 questions before building, build a personal automation first
- CTA: Follow CTA or Bridge CTA (50% of the time)
- Leading indicator: saves

**Type 3 — Barrier/Reframe**
Collapses a false belief and replaces it with a concrete action. Combines old barrier removal, myth busting, reframe, and first step types into one.

- Hook patterns: "You don't need [X] to [Y], but you do need this." / "Everyone thinks [wrong]. Here's what's true." / "This is what [X] actually involves (it's not what you think)."
- Examples: you don't need a CS degree, learn skills not platforms, building apps is planning not coding, AI does the hard part
- CTA: Always Follow CTA (never Bridge)
- Leading indicator: shares

### Format 2: Story Reels (Share Machine)

Stories show. They let the viewer feel what life looks like when you build with AI. Use Structure B.

**Type 4 — Lifestyle Moment**
Shows the outcome of building with AI: freedom, flexibility, fun. Travis as living proof.

- Hook patterns: scene + unexpected connection, person + unexpected action, unexpected location + building, casual flex
- Examples: building from a rooftop, coding at a coffee shop abroad, app notification while hiking, casual "this is my Tuesday"
- CTA: Always Follow CTA
- Leading indicator: shares + likes

**Type 5 — Milestone/Event**
Hackathon, app launch, user milestone, surprise outcome. Real moments of achievement.

- Hook patterns: milestone + what happened next, "I just..." + unexpected result, event moment + reaction
- Examples: won a hackathon, first paying user, app hit 1K users, built something in 5 hours that works
- CTA: Always Follow CTA
- Leading indicator: shares + likes

## BLUEPRINT FRESHNESS FRAMEWORK

Blueprints only work when they contain genuinely new information. Repeating the same stack/workflow to saturation kills save rates and follows.

### 3-Question Freshness Test

Before creating any blueprint reel, it must pass all three:

1. **Have I actually done this?** The blueprint must come from personal experience, not theory.
2. **Is this new for my audience?** If you've made a reel covering the same territory, the new one must add something the old one didn't.
3. **Does it contain something from the last 3 months?** At least one element (tool, technique, result) should be current enough that it couldn't have been made 6 months ago.

### Blueprint Territory Map

| Territory | Status | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Cheapest stack to build an app | Saturated | Multiple reels covering Cursor + free tiers |
| "You don't need to code" barrier removal | Saturated | Core message, well-covered |
| "Learn skills not platforms" reframe | Saturated | Strong reel exists, don't repeat |
| Personal automation workflow | Untapped | How Travis automates his own life with AI-built tools |
| Idea to first paying user | Untapped | End-to-end journey, not just "build an MVP" |
| AI at your 9-to-5 vs side projects | Untapped | Different contexts, different stacks, different outcomes |
| Build + ship a specific app with outcome | Untapped | "I built X, it does Y, here's what happened" |
| Testing/debugging workflow | Untapped | What to do when the AI-built app breaks |
| How to evaluate if your idea is buildable | Untapped | Pre-building framework |

### How to Generate New Blueprints

1. Start from personal experience — what did you build, fix, or automate recently?
2. Check the territory map — is this fresh or saturated?
3. Frame it as a complete system — not a tip, but a referenceable process
4. If the territory is saturated, find a new angle or skip it

## BRIDGE CTA STRATEGY

Bridge CTAs move viewers from Instagram to Substack. They replace the follow CTA (never stack both in the same reel).

### Bridge Formula

"I break this down step by step in my newsletter. Link in bio."

Variations:
- "I wrote the full breakdown with screenshots in my newsletter. Link in bio."
- "If you want the complete walkthrough, I put it in my newsletter. Link in bio."

### When to Use Bridge CTAs

| Reel Type | Bridge CTA? | Why |
|-----------|-------------|-----|
| Stack/Workflow | Always | These naturally extend into detailed Substack content |
| How-To/Process | 50% of the time | Use when the process has more depth than 30 seconds covers |
| Barrier/Reframe | Never | These are belief shifts, not reference material |
| Lifestyle Moment | Never | Story reels sell the person, not the newsletter |
| Milestone/Event | Never | Story reels sell the person, not the newsletter |

### Targets

- 2-3 bridge CTAs per week across blueprint reels
- Track newsletter signups as the bridge CTA performance indicator
- Bridge CTA replaces the follow CTA for that reel — never use both

## B-ROLL CONTENT GUIDE

B-roll reels are text-based: one complete thought displayed as text over footage. The text IS the reel. Short, punchy, tweet-like.

### The #1 Rule for B-Roll

**Speak directly TO your target person.** The best b-roll hooks name the audience or address their specific situation. The worst ones talk abstractly about AI. Every hook should make a founder, entrepreneur, or person with an idea think "this is about me."

### What Works as B-Roll (Ranked by Performance)

1. **Barrier removal with a flip** — "You don't need X. You need Y." Directly addresses the audience's blockers and gives them the real answer.
   - "You don't need a developer, a co-founder, or a CS degree. You need an AI coding agent and a clear plan."
2. **Simple step-by-step** — Makes building feel achievable and immediate. Numbered steps, then close the loop.
   - "Step 1: describe your idea to Claude. Step 2: build it with an AI coding agent. There is no step 3."
3. **Concrete before/after with numbers** — Shows the shift with real numbers ($20, weekend, $50K). Not abstract.
   - "Entrepreneurs are shipping MVPs in weekends. That used to take months and a technical co-founder."
4. **Nuanced reframes** — Shifts a mental model without being aggressive or preachy.
   - "AI didn't make building easy. It made building accessible. There's a difference."
5. **Familiar analogies** — Compares building apps to something they already understand.
   - "Building apps with AI is like learning Excel. It's not hard, it's just something you need to learn."
6. **Identity statements** — Tells the audience who the builders are (and it's them).
   - "The people building apps with AI right now aren't all engineers. They're founders, entrepreneurs, and people with ideas."

### How to Write B-Roll Text

1. Start with the target person's situation or belief, not with AI
2. Make it declarative. No "I think" or "maybe"
3. Use concrete numbers where possible ($20, weekend, hours)
4. Test: Would someone who has never heard of "vibe coding" screenshot this?
5. Keep it under 20 words when possible. Never use em dashes. Use periods for sentence breaks.

### What NOT to Write (Common Mistakes)

- **Competitive/confrontational framing** — "ships faster than a funded team with a pitch deck." Sounds combative, not inviting.
- **Fear/urgency tactics** — "everyone else is late." Pushes people away instead of pulling them in.
- **Abstract AI mechanics** — "AI handles the code. You handle the decisions." Talks about AI, not to the person.
- **Directive/bossy tone** — "Stop researching tools." Feels preachy.
- **Debate-y comparisons** — "Same excuse, different decade." Sounds like a Twitter argument.
- **Self-focused anecdotes** — "I built an app that does X." Unless it ties directly to their outcome, it's about you, not them.
- **Generic motivational language** — "The barrier is gone." Too vague to stop a scroll.
- **Tool stack lists** — These get saves from the existing audience but don't reach new people at TOF.

## PROVEN HOOK FORMULAS

### Blueprint Hook Formulas

Extracted from highest-performing reels by saves and shares.

| Formula | Example |
|---------|---------|
| **This is the biggest mistake [audience] makes when [action].** | "This is the biggest mistake entrepreneurs make when trying to build apps." |
| **If you have [relatable situation], [directive].** | "If you have an app idea but never learned to code, this changes everything." |
| **These are the [#] [things] I would [do] if [situation].** | "These are the 3 things I'd learn if I wanted to build apps without coding." |
| **You don't need [X] to [Y], but you do need this.** | "You don't need to code to build apps, but you do need this." |
| **This is the [fastest/simplest/only] way to [outcome].** | "This is the cheapest way to build and launch an app. Under $50." |
| **This is why [surprising/counterintuitive outcome].** | "This is why learning to build apps is the best career insurance right now." |
| **This is [strong claim] and no, [dispel common disbelief].** | "This is the best career insurance you can get right now against AI and no, it's not another degree or certification." |

**Never write a hook that:** leads with an opinion/prediction, sounds philosophical, uses insider jargon, assumes they know "vibe coding"

**Always write hooks that:** lead with a mistake/directive/list/reframe/gap, speak to business outcomes, use language entrepreneurs already use, open a loop

**Preferred talking head hook pattern:** Open with a strong claim, then immediately dispel the most common misconception for that topic. Use "and no," to bridge. Example: "This is the fastest way to start building apps right now and no, you don't need to learn to code first." This works because it opens a loop (what IS it then?) and removes the viewer's first objection before they can scroll away.

### Story Hook Formulas

| Formula | Example |
|---------|---------|
| **Scene + unexpected connection** | "I'm building an app from a rooftop in Mexico City." |
| **Person + unexpected action** | "My app just got its first paying user while I was at the gym." |
| **Unexpected location + building action** | "Debugging production from a hammock." |
| **Casual flex** | "This is what my Tuesday looks like since I started building with AI." |
| **Milestone + what happened** | "I built this in 5 hours at a hackathon. It now has 200 users." |

## THE TOF GATE (3-Question Filter)

Run every topic and hook through this before using. If it fails any one, rewrite or cut.

| # | Question | Blueprint Pass | Story Pass | Fail |
|---|----------|---------------|------------|------|
| 1 | Would someone who has NEVER heard of "vibe coding" stop scrolling? | Hook speaks to a universal pain | Hook shows a moment they're drawn to | Uses insider jargon, targets mid-journey audience |
| 2 | Does it connect to something they already want? | Removes a barrier before they discover building with AI | Shows an outcome they already want (freedom, flexibility, status) | Solves problems only someone already building would have |
| 3 | Does it make them want to follow YOU for more? | Promises next step, shows proof, creates value gap | Makes them want to see more of this life | Video is complete, no reason to follow |
