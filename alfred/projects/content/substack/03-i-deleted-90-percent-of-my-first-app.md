Subject: I deleted 90% of my first app

I need to tell you about the first app I built with AI because I think it'll save you a lot of time.

I had an idea for a personal finance tracker. Simple enough, right? Track expenses, see where money goes, maybe some charts.

I opened up my tools and started building. Within an hour I had a basic expense tracker working. Cool.

Then I kept going.

I added categories. Then subcategories. Then a dashboard with charts. Then a budget planner. Then recurring expense tracking. Then CSV export. Then a dark mode because why not.

Two days later I had an app with 15+ features and I hadn't even used it once to track a single expense.

I stepped back, looked at what I built, and realized I'd been building for the sake of building. The app was bloated. Half the features didn't work well together. The thing I actually needed — a simple way to see where my money was going — was buried under a mountain of stuff I didn't need.

So I deleted almost everything. Kept the expense input, the category tags, and one chart. That's it.

And you know what? That stripped-down version is the one I actually use every day.

### Why this happens to everyone

When building is fast, your brain tricks you. Every feature feels like it'll only take 10 minutes. And it does. But 10 minutes times 30 features is 5 hours of building and a mess of an app.

The speed of AI makes discipline more important, not less.

I see this with everyone who starts building. Entrepreneurs are especially vulnerable because you're wired to see opportunity everywhere. "Oh it could also do this. And this. And what if we added this for enterprise clients."

Slow down.

### What I do differently now

Before I build anything, I write down the one thing the app does. Then I build only that. I don't even let myself think about "phase 2" features until phase 1 is done and I've used it for at least a week.

If I find myself reaching for more features, I stop and ask: "Am I building this because I need it, or because I can?"

That question alone has saved me days.

### The real lesson

The hardest part of building with AI isn't the building. It's the restraint.

Anyone can generate a mountain of features. The skill is knowing which ones matter and having the discipline to stop there.

Build the smallest useful thing. Use it. Then decide what's missing based on real experience, not imagination.
